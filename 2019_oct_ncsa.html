Key Project: Waterlogging Detection in Champaign County

One of my core contributions involved building a deep learning model to detect waterlogged farmland in Champaign County using hyperspectral imagery from the PlanetLab CubeSat. The agricultural sector faces significant challenges when it comes to waterlogging, which negatively impacts crop yields. This project focused on identifying waterlogged areas to assess their effect on agricultural productivity.

  • Dataset: Worked with ~250 CubeSat images, approximately 40GB of 3m resolution imagery, manually labeling the waterlogged areas.
  • Model: Developed a U-Net architecture in Pytorch that achieved 95% accuracy in segmenting waterlogged farmland.
  • Scaling: Scaled the solution to cover hundreds of counties across Illinois, analyzing hundreds of gigabytes of GIS data.

Waterlogging segmentation using U-Net on CubeSat data

Key Project: Geometric Correction on High-Resolution Satellite Imagery

In a second project, I tackled the challenge of geometric correction for high-resolution satellite images. These images, crucial for precision agriculture, often suffer from locational drift and distortion due to imperfect extrinsic calibration. The goal was to correct this drift through advanced computer vision and geospatial techniques.

  • Challenges: The system lacked reliable locational accuracy due to calibration drift, causing distortions in GIS-based agricultural data.
  • Solution: Applied a combination of SIFT and RANSAC computer vision algorithms for feature matching, followed by geospatial transformations to achieve sub-meter accuracy.
  • Impact: Improved the reliability and accuracy of satellite imagery for precision farming applications requiring exact geographic information.

Pre and post geometric correction of high-resolution GIS data

Challenges

One of the most difficult aspects of the internship was dealing with the sheer scale of the data, which often required complex optimizations in both computation and memory handling. The combination of remote sensing techniques and machine learning, though effective, had its hurdles due to noise and variability in satellite data. Additionally, working with multi-channel TIF files for hyperspectral analysis added another layer of complexity in terms of data preprocessing and interpretation.

Technologies Used

  • Programming: Python, Pytorch, OpenCV
  • Remote Sensing Tools: GIS, Sentinel-2, CubeSat
  • Techniques: Machine Learning, CNNs, SIFT, RANSAC, Geometric Transformations
  • Platforms: Cloud Computing, GNSS, Git

This experience laid the foundation for further exploration into the application of remote sensing and AI in agriculture. Both the waterlogging detection and geometric correction projects showcased the potential of modern ML/AI techniques in resolving real-world agricultural challenges.
